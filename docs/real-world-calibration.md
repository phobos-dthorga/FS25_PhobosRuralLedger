# Real-World Calibration

The real-life basis should be broad rather than overly precise. The design seed
mentions USDA, ABARES, and similar farm-finance categories as useful reference
shapes, not exact balance-sheet targets.

## Useful Category Families

- income;
- expenses;
- debt;
- assets;
- government payments;
- commodity outlook;
- land value;
- risk and disasters;
- farm performance by region, industry, and farm size.

## Gameplay Translation

| Real category | Gameplay use |
| --- | --- |
| Income | Crop, livestock, contract, production, rent, subsidy, and insurance revenue. |
| Expenses | Seed, fertiliser, chemical, fuel, labour, rent, maintenance, depreciation, and tax pressure. |
| Debt | Interest pressure, forced sales, delayed work, or conservative behavior. |
| Assets | Land, storage, machinery, livestock, and production capacity. |
| Commodity outlook | Local market board pressure and future crop-choice bias. |
| Land value | Expansion pressure, lease attractiveness, and auction intensity. |
| Disaster support | Insurance or government relief events after drought, flood, disease, or price collapse. |
| Regional performance | Broad preset differences between grain, dairy, livestock, rice, and mixed farming maps. |

## Calibration Principle

Use real categories to make the system feel plausible, then tune for gameplay.
The player should experience believable pressure and opportunity without needing
to audit a farm business statement.

## Abstraction Rules

- Use ranges and indices instead of exact real-world costs.
- Prefer per-hectare and per-enterprise estimates over detailed invoices.
- Tune outputs for interesting player decisions.
- Keep regional presets broad and legible.
- Record any real dataset used for balancing in this file or a follow-up doc.

## Candidate Regional Presets

- US Midwest grain;
- Australian broadacre;
- EU mixed farming;
- Alpine dairy;
- rice region;
- livestock-heavy region;
- high land-value peri-urban region.

## Starter Regional Differences

| Preset | Bias |
| --- | --- |
| US Midwest grain | Larger arable farms, stronger grain price sensitivity, moderate storage focus. |
| Australian broadacre | Larger fields, drought exposure, export pressure, high machinery logistics. |
| EU mixed farming | Smaller holdings, policy support, mixed crop/livestock pressure. |
| Alpine dairy | Dairy margins, hay and silage demand, limited land expansion. |
| Rice region | Water and timing pressure, specialized crop risk, strong seasonal rhythm. |
